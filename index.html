Succulent plants,also known as succulents or sometimes water storage plants

That are plants that have some parts that are more than normally thickened and fleshy, usually to retain water in arid climates or soil conditions.The word "succulent" comes from the Latin word sucus, meaning juice, or sap. Succulent plants may store water in various structures, such as leaves and stems.

The storage of water often gives succulent
plants a more swollen or fleshy appearance than other plants,
a characteristic known as succulence.
